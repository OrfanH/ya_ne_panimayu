# STORY.md — Один Семестр: Full Story Bible

## Premise

You are a foreign student — nationality unspecified, language of origin unspecified — who has just arrived in Малинов (Malinov), a small Russian town. You have a semester exchange placement at the local university. You know almost no Russian. You have an address written on a crumpled piece of paper, a phone with a dead battery, and no idea where anything is.

The story is not about saving the world. It is about surviving a Tuesday. Then a Wednesday. Then realizing, somewhere around week six, that you understand the woman at the market without reading the subtitles.

---

## The town: Малинов (Malinov)

A real Russian small town — not a postcard, not a cliche. Concrete apartment blocks beside old wooden houses. A market that smells of dill and diesel. A park where old men play chess and teenagers sit on benches. A cafe that has been there since 1987 and sees no reason to change. A train station that connects Malinov to everywhere, but most people never leave.

The university is mentioned but never seen. It is the excuse that brought you here.

## Chapter structure

| Chapter | Locations | Story arc |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Apartment building | Arriving. Finding your feet. Meeting one person who helps you. |
| 2 | Park | Cautious exploration. The town starts to feel like a place. |
| 3 | Cafe + Market | You need things. You learn to ask. Embarrassing moments. Small victories. |
| 4 | Train station + Police station | Something goes wrong. You have to navigate bureaucracy and urgency in a language you barely know. |

## Chapter arcs

### Chapter 1 — Arrival
The player arrives with nothing: no Russian, no bearings, no social network. The apartment building is the whole world. Galina Ivanovna makes it navigable. Styopan makes it livable. The player ends the chapter able to say who they are, where they live, and what they need.

Story beat: player is trying to find their apartment. Gets it wrong twice. Finally finds it. Galina Ivanovna is in the hallway. She doesn't make it a big deal. She just helps.

Unlock gate: Galina Ivanovna tells player the park is worth visiting. She mentions Artyom by name. "He's a good boy. He knows where things are."

### Chapter 2 — First steps
The town becomes something more than walls. The park opens the world spatially — player can now move, describe, orient. Artyom shows them the shape of the town. Tamara Andreyevna gives them their first sense of Russian culture beyond survival.

Story beat: player gets turned around in the park. Artyom finds them, not quite by accident. They have their first real conversation. It is mostly pointing and nodding, but something is communicated.

Unlock gate: Artyom explicitly tells player about the cafe and the market. "If you're going to be here all semester, you need to know where to eat." He gives them directions in Russian. They mostly follow them.

### Chapter 3 — Needing things
The player has to interact with the world commercially. The cafe and market require them to ask for things, pay for things, describe things. Mistakes have social consequences (mild ones: embarrassment, confusion) but also social rewards.

Story beat: player attempts to order at the cafe. Gets it almost right. Lena corrects them and gives them what they wanted anyway. Player feels, for the first time, that they can do this.

Second story beat: at the market, Fatima will not sell without being asked properly. Player has to figure out how to ask. She waits. She does not help until they try. When they get it right, she says "хорошо" ("good") and means it.

Unlock gate: something is wrong. The player needs to go to the train station. Styopan is the one who tells them. It is not dramatic — just a practical problem that requires going somewhere new. "You might need to go to the station. Konstantin there can help."

### Chapter 4 — Something goes wrong
The player has lost a document. Or a bag. Something official needs to happen. The train station and police station are where formal Russian lives. The chapter is about navigating bureaucracy in a foreign language — which is something most language learners will eventually face in real life.

Story beat at station: Konstantin helps the player track down what happened to their document. He is efficient and calm. Player has to describe the problem, give times, give dates.

Story beat at police station: Viktor takes the report. Player has to tell the story of what happened in past tense. Alina processes the paperwork. The situation is resolved — not dramatically, just resolved. The player realizes they handled it. In Russian.

Final beat: player returns to Galina Ivanovna. She asks how it went. Player tells her. She nods and goes back to her watering can. "Good. Have you eaten?"

The semester is not over. But the player has arrived.

## Vocabulary scope documentation

*(Supporting the curriculum-map.md that curriculum-designer will write)*

**Location 1 — Apartment building:**
Greetings (привет, здравствуйте, добрый день), farewells, self-introduction (меня зовут, я из, я студент/студентка), numbers 1-10, floor/apartment vocabulary, polite words (пожалуйста, спасибо, извините), basic yes/no (да/нет), "I don't understand" (я не понимаю)

**Location 2 — Park:**
Directions (налево, направо, прямо, здесь, там), adjectives (big/small, new/old, beautiful), colors (basic 6), simple present tense (я иду, он сидит), question words (где, что, как, кто), weather basics, describing people and places

**Location 3 — Cafe:**
Ordering vocabulary (я хочу, дайте мне, можно), drink and food names, prices (numbers 10-100), accusative case unlabelled (I want coffee → кофе), polite request forms (можно мне, не могли бы вы), "how much" (сколько стоит)

**Location 4 — Market:**
Produce vocabulary, quantity expressions (один, полкило, два кило), more colors, adjectives for quality (свежий, спелый, хороший), "I'll take this/that," paying and change, "I don't want/need" (мне не нужно)

**Location 5 — Train station:**
Time expressions (в котором часу, во сколько, через час), future tense (поезд прибудет в), direction in buildings (первый путь, второй вагон), ticket vocabulary, days of the week, formal instruction-following language

**Location 6 — Police station:**
Formal register (вы vs ты established explicitly), past tense narrative (я потерял/а, это произошло), document vocabulary (паспорт, документ, адрес), case awareness (genitive after у меня нет), "I need" (мне нужно + infinitive), emergency phrases, formal goodbyes

Reinforcement chain: every vocabulary domain introduced in location N appears in at least one interaction at location N+1.
