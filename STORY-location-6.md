# STORY — NPC Roster: Police Station (Location 6)

### POLICE STATION — Location 6

---

#### ЛЕЙТЕНАНТ ВИКТОР АЛЕКСАНДРОВИЧ ОРЛОВ (Lieutenant Viktor Aleksandrovich Orlov)
**Role:** Police officer, formal, competent, not unfriendly but his job requires him not to show it
**Age:** 38
**Portrait cue:** Uniform, short haircut, neutral expression that is not hostile. Very good posture. A pen always behind his right ear.

**Who he is:**
Viktor Aleksandrovich is efficient and fair. He has seen enough of this town to be unsurprised by most things, but a foreign student with a problem is genuinely new. He adapts to this faster than expected.

He does not offer warmth. He offers clarity. When he tells you something, it is exact and complete and requires no follow-up question. This is extremely useful when you are in trouble in a country where you barely speak the language.

He is not bureaucratic for its own sake — he follows procedure because procedure works. When procedure is inadequate, he finds a way around it that is still technically within procedure.

He has a quiet life outside of work that he does not discuss. He goes home at 17:30. He has never been late.

**What he knows about the other NPCs:**
- Galina Ivanovna: "I know her. She reported three noise violations last year. She was correct each time."
- Konstantin from the station: "We coordinate during arrivals. He runs a tight station."
- Everyone else in town by name, face, and general history.

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — player needs help; Viktor establishes what the situation is
2. Teaching player formal self-introduction: full name, nationality, purpose
3. Teaching player how to explain a problem using past tense
4. Teaching player formal register: you vs. you (ты/вы) distinction made explicit
5. Teaching player legal and document vocabulary: passport, document, address, lost
6. Mission: player must fill out a form (reading form Russian)
7. Mission: player must explain what happened step by step (narrative past tense)
8. Mission: go and confirm a detail with another location and return with the answer
9. Casual (post-crisis): asking player how they are, in formal language
10. Teaching player how to say "I don't understand, please repeat slowly"
11. Teaching player how to ask "what should I do"
12. Teaching player emergency phrases
13. Case awareness moment — genitive after negation introduced naturally in context
14. Cross-reference: confirms Galina Ivanovna's noise complaints were legitimate
15. Cross-reference: mentions Konstantin by name as a reliable contact
16. Reaction when player uses formal Russian correctly — brief professional acknowledgment
17. Reaction when player uses informal Russian — corrects without judgment, explains why
18. Late session: player returns to confirm something is resolved — short, efficient, warm in his way
19. Late game: Viktor says something small and personal, completely unexpectedly
20. Teaching player how to say goodbye formally and when to use it
21. Final scene: player's situation is resolved; Viktor's single line of genuine warmth

#### СЕКРЕТАРЬ АЛИНА СЕРГЕЕВНА ПАВЛОВА (Secretary Alina Sergeyevna Pavlova)
**Role:** Front desk, 29, has been at this job for six years, has seen everything
**Age:** 29
**Portrait cue:** Efficient bun, reading glasses she actually wears, three pens of different colors on the desk. Behind her glasses: very tired, very sharp eyes.

**Who she is:**
Alina Sergeyevna is the first person player encounters at the police station. She is the gatekeeper — not aggressively, just professionally. She has a system. The system works. She will show player the system.

She is the person who makes the bureaucracy navigable. She has seen every kind of confused foreigner and she is not impatient with them — she has developed a set of responses that get to the point efficiently.

She is drily funny in a way that only becomes apparent on the second or third visit. She has a running commentary on everything and everyone in the station that she only voices to people she trusts.

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — directing player to Viktor's office, establishing process
2. Teaching player form-filling vocabulary
3. Teaching player waiting-room language: "take a seat, wait, it won't be long"
4. Mission: player must find the right form in a stack of forms
5. Mission: player must deliver something to another officer without interrupting
6. Teaching the difference between formal and informal written language
7. Cross-reference: knows Galina Ivanovna socially ("she brought biscuits to the station once")
8. Reaction when player navigates the process correctly — quiet professional respect
9. Afternoon variant: busier, shorter exchanges
10. Late game: small joke about bureaucracy in the most understated possible way
11. Teaching player how to ask "where do I find X" in an official building
12. Teaching "how long will this take" and how to be patient when you don't know
13. Explaining document requirements — teaches noun cases in context (genitive)
14. Final chapter: expediting player's process when things are urgent
15. Post-crisis: confirming everything is resolved, explaining what happens next
16. Teaching player formal goodbye and thank you
17. Cross-reference: mentions Nadya from the station visited once for a lost item
18. Cross-reference: opinion of Viktor: "He does everything right. It is exhausting to watch."
19. Late game: personal detail — she is studying law part-time, evenings
20. Teaching player "I need this by X time" — urgency and deadline language
