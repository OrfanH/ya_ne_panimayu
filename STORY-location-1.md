# STORY — NPC Roster: Apartment Building (Location 1)

### APARTMENT BUILDING — Location 1

---

#### ГАЛИНА ИВАНОВНА СОКОЛОВА (Galina Ivanovna Sokolova)
**Role:** Neighbor, de facto building manager, first teacher
**Age:** 68
**Portrait cue:** Round face, silver hair pulled back, reading glasses pushed up on her forehead. Always wearing a floral housecoat. Perpetually holding something — a watering can, a jar of jam, a key ring.

**Who she is:**
Galina Ivanovna has lived in apartment 3 for 41 years. She knew everyone in this building before they moved in and after they left. She is not nosy — she is thorough. There is a difference. She lost her husband seven years ago and her daughter moved to Voronezh, so the building has become her project. She takes quiet satisfaction in being useful.

She is warm but direct. She will not soften things to spare your feelings. If you say something wrong, she will repeat it back to you correctly with a slight nod, as if confirming a fact. She is not unkind — she simply respects you enough to be accurate.

She has strong opinions about soup, about youth, about the correct way to store vegetables, and about a man named Dmitry Petrovich on the fourth floor (she does not approve of his music). She does not gossip. She reports.

**What she knows about the other NPCs:**
- She has seen Artyom from the park since he was a child. She approves of him. She thinks he needs a proper meal.
- She buys her bread from Fatima at the market and considers her the only honest vendor.
- She is suspicious of Lena from the cafe. "Too much eyeliner for a Tuesday."

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — player knocks on wrong door, she redirects them to their apartment
2. Teaching player their apartment number and floor
3. Explaining the building rules (no noise after 10pm, recycling in the courtyard)
4. Offering tea and teaching "yes/no/please/thank you"
5. Asking where the player is from and reacting with genuine curiosity
6. Teaching numbers 1-10 when explaining the intercom code
7. Showing player how to read the mailbox numbers
8. Giving player a spare key and explaining "lose it and I will not replace it"
9. Return visit: asking if player ate (teaching "I am hungry/full")
10. Return visit: teaching player to introduce themselves properly
11. Mission: deliver jam to apartment 7 (teaching floor numbers and knocking etiquette)
12. Mission: tell her what Dmitry Petrovich's music sounded like last night (teaching descriptions)
13. Mission: find out if the post arrived (teaching "is there/there is no")
14. Casual: showing player her plants and naming them (colors, adjectives)
15. Casual: talking about her daughter in Voronezh (family words, city names)
16. Chapter 2 reference: asking if player went to the park yet, mentions Artyom
17. Chapter 3 reference: recommending Fatima's stall at the market
18. Reaction to player making a grammar mistake — gentle correction, no embarrassment
19. Reaction to player making unexpected progress — quiet pride, no fuss
20. Seasonal: commenting on weather changing, teaching basic weather phrases
21. Late game: asking player to read a note for her (reversing teacher/student dynamic)
22. Late game: telling player about why she stayed in Malinov when everyone else left
23. Final chapter: worried when player is in trouble, sends them to the police station herself

#### СТЕПАН (Styopan)
**Role:** Player's upstairs neighbor, age 24, student at the same university
**Age:** 24
**Portrait cue:** Tall, tired-looking, always in a university hoodie. Headphones usually around his neck, never on his ears.

**Who he is:**
Styopan is from Yekaterinburg. He has been in Malinov for two years and has developed the specific world-weariness of someone who chose to study here and is not sure that was right. He is not unfriendly — he is simply low-energy. He speaks to you like you are already an acquaintance, which is its own kind of warmth.

He is studying economics and finds it boring. He plays video games badly and talks about them constantly. He knows where everything in the building is and what is broken and why. He has a complicated relationship with Galina Ivanovna (she once confiscated his delivery package for making noise, and he has never fully forgiven her).

He will occasionally appear at the wrong moment and say exactly the wrong thing in a way that is accidentally useful to the player.

**What he knows about the other NPCs:**
- Tells player about Artyom in the park: "He's always there. I think the park is his apartment."
- Mentions Lena at the cafe: "Best coffee in town. Which is not saying much."
- Doesn't know anyone at the train station personally but has strong opinions about the schedule.

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting in the hallway — startled to see a new face
2. Explaining that the elevator is broken (and has been for six months)
3. Teaching player the word for stairs, floor, door
4. Asking player what courses they are taking (teaching subjects and schedules)
5. Complaining about Galina Ivanovna — player learns conflict language
6. Asking player to translate something from English on his phone (role reversal)
7. Mission: slide note under neighbor's door (teaching "please be quiet")
8. Mission: check if anyone is using the laundry room
9. Casual: showing player his video game and asking if they know it
10. Casual: explaining Russian university culture vs. the player's home country
11. Casual: complaining about the cafeteria (teaching food words negatively)
12. Chapter 2: asking if player has been outside yet, slightly judging them
13. Chapter 3: recommending the cafe but getting Lena's name wrong ("the one with the eyes")
14. Chapter 4: player sees him stressed about an exam — teaches "I have to, I need to"
15. Teaching player slang the moment Galina Ivanovna is out of earshot
16. Reaction to player learning a new word: "Oh, she taught you that?"
17. Reaction to player being late back: mild concern disguised as annoyance
18. Reference to the train station: "I went home once. The train took nine hours."
19. Late game: admitting he is glad they moved in — but said very casually
20. Late game: asking player to help him write a note in English to someone
21. Final chapter: the one who tells player there is a problem and they need to go to the police station
