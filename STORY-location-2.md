# STORY — NPC Roster: Park (Location 2)

### PARK — Location 2

---

#### АРТЁМ ВОРОНОВ (Artyom Voronov)
**Role:** The young man who is always in the park, informal guide to the town
**Age:** 21
**Portrait cue:** Lean, quick eyes, usually sitting on the back of a bench rather than the seat. Always has a paperback book in his back pocket that he is not currently reading.

**Who he is:**
Artyom grew up in Malinov and has complicated feelings about still being there. He finished school two years ago and has been working at the small grocery kiosk near the park entrance while figuring out what to do next. He reads a lot — mostly Soviet-era adventure novels and whatever he can find translated into Russian. He is curious about the world in the way of someone who has not left their town yet.

He speaks clearly and relatively slowly, which makes him the best conversationalist the player will encounter early on. He is not performing patience — he is genuinely interested in the player and wants to understand them. He will ask questions the player cannot yet answer, and find this amusing rather than frustrating.

He knows everyone in this town. Not in the social way — in the way of someone who has watched the same streets for twenty-one years.

**What he knows about the other NPCs:**
- Galina Ivanovna: "She fed me every summer when I was small. I still owe her about forty jars of jam."
- Fatima at the market: "Buy from her, not from the guy next to her. Trust me."
- Lena at the cafe: "She's been there forever. She knows things about this town she'll never tell you."
- Konstantin at the station: "He's all right. Just don't ask him things he doesn't know — he'll make something up."

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — he notices player looking lost on the park path
2. Teaching player basic directions: left, right, straight ahead, here, there
3. Describing the park — benches, fountain, trees (adjectives, simple present)
4. Asking player yes/no questions about their life — modeling question structure
5. Teaching player how to describe the weather using what they observe
6. Mission: find the old man playing chess (teaches "where is" + location words)
7. Mission: deliver a message to someone at the far end of the park
8. Mission: help him understand something a tourist asked him in English
9. Casual: showing player which path leads to the cafe, which to the market
10. Casual: talking about his book and what it is about (narrative tense practice)
11. Casual: pointing out regulars in the park and describing them (descriptions)
12. Chapter 3 bridge: walks player toward the cafe, introduces them to Lena indirectly
13. Chapter 3 bridge: mentions Fatima's market specifically — "Tell her Artyom sent you"
14. Reaction to player using a direction correctly — genuine delight
15. Reaction to player describing something in Russian unprompted
16. Rainy day variant: player finds him under the covered area with a different mood
17. Seasonal variant: summer afternoon vs. early autumn afternoon — different energy
18. Cross-reference: asks if Galina Ivanovna is feeding player properly
19. Cross-reference: tells player about the train station before player has unlocked it
20. Late game: reveals he has applied to a university in another city
21. Late game: asks player what their city is like — player describes home in Russian
22. Final chapter: concerned when player is in trouble, offers to come with them (can't)
23. Secret background: the book in his pocket is one Galina Ivanovna gave him years ago

#### ТАМАРА АНДРЕЕВНА БОРИСОВА (Tamara Andreyevna Borisova)
**Role:** Retired schoolteacher, regular park visitor, chess-adjacent
**Age:** 71
**Portrait cue:** Upright posture, warm eyes behind small rectangular glasses. Always in a coat regardless of the temperature. Often sitting near the chess tables, watching.

**Who she is:**
Tamara Andreyevna taught Russian language and literature for 35 years. She retired six years ago and now she comes to the park every day because routine is what keeps her sharp. She is not melancholy about retirement — she is quite satisfied. She taught well. She left something behind.

She will immediately recognize the player as a language learner and will feel the professional reflex to help. But she does this gently, in the style of someone who has spent decades finding the right way to explain the same thing to thirty different people. She gives the player the sense that making mistakes is a normal and temporary condition.

She knows a great deal about the history of Malinov and will share it unprompted but not boringly.

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — greets player with formal Russian, then adjusts when they look confused
2. Teaching player formal vs. informal greetings
3. Explaining that she was a teacher — player learns job words
4. Mission: she has lost her reading glasses somewhere in the park
5. Mission: she wants player to describe what the fountain looks like in Russian
6. Casual: talking about the seasons in Russia (comparative adjectives)
7. Casual: explaining the chess game she is watching without teaching chess
8. Cross-reference: mentions Galina Ivanovna as "a woman with a good heart and very strong opinions"
9. Cross-reference: knows Artyom's mother and asks after him
10. Teaching player formal address forms (Ivanovna, Andreyevna — why names work this way)
11. Reaction when player uses formal address correctly — visible approval
12. Teaching colors using the flowers planted near the fountain
13. Correcting a grammar mistake with a complete explanation, warm not clinical
14. Explaining a Russian saying and what it actually means
15. Chapter 3 crossover: mentions she sometimes goes to the cafe on Saturdays
16. Telling player about the town's history — old names, what was here before
17. Seasonal variant: autumn — talks about the trees, nostalgia, Russian poetry (first lines)
18. Late game: asks player to read something for her — she wants to hear their Russian
19. Late game: tells player they remind her of a particular student she had years ago
20. Final chapter: if player is in trouble, she is the one who tells them to stay calm
