# STORY.md — Один Семестр: Full Story Bible

## Premise

You are a foreign student — nationality unspecified, language of origin unspecified — who has just arrived in Малинов (Malinov), a small Russian town. You have a semester exchange placement at the local university. You know almost no Russian. You have an address written on a crumpled piece of paper, a phone with a dead battery, and no idea where anything is.

The story is not about saving the world. It is about surviving a Tuesday. Then a Wednesday. Then realizing, somewhere around week six, that you understand the woman at the market without reading the subtitles.

---

## The town: Малинов (Malinov)

A real Russian small town — not a postcard, not a cliche. Concrete apartment blocks beside old wooden houses. A market that smells of dill and diesel. A park where old men play chess and teenagers sit on benches. A cafe that has been there since 1987 and sees no reason to change. A train station that connects Malinov to everywhere, but most people never leave.

The university is mentioned but never seen. It is the excuse that brought you here.

---

## Chapter structure

| Chapter | Locations | Story arc |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Apartment building | Arriving. Finding your feet. Meeting one person who helps you. |
| 2 | Park | Cautious exploration. The town starts to feel like a place. |
| 3 | Cafe + Market | You need things. You learn to ask. Embarrassing moments. Small victories. |
| 4 | Train station + Police station | Something goes wrong. You have to navigate bureaucracy and urgency in a language you barely know. |

#### ГАЛИНА ИВАНОВНА СОКОЛОВА (Galina Ivanovna Sokolova)
**Role:** Neighbor, de facto building manager, first teacher
**Age:** 68
**Portrait cue:** Round face, silver hair pulled back, reading glasses pushed up on her forehead. Always wearing a floral housecoat. Perpetually holding something — a watering can, a jar of jam, a key ring.

**Who she is:**
Galina Ivanovna has lived in apartment 3 for 41 years. She knew everyone in this building before they moved in and after they left. She is not nosy — she is thorough. There is a difference. She lost her husband seven years ago and her daughter moved to Voronezh, so the building has become her project. She takes quiet satisfaction in being useful.

She is warm but direct. She will not soften things to spare your feelings. If you say something wrong, she will repeat it back to you correctly with a slight nod, as if confirming a fact. She is not unkind — she simply respects you enough to be accurate.

She has strong opinions about soup, about youth, about the correct way to store vegetables, and about a man named Dmitry Petrovich on the fourth floor (she does not approve of his music). She does not gossip. She reports.

**What she knows about the other NPCs:**
- She has seen Artyom from the park since he was a child. She approves of him. She thinks he needs a proper meal.
- She buys her bread from Fatima at the market and considers her the only honest vendor.
- She is suspicious of Lena from the cafe. "Too much eyeliner for a Tuesday."

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — player knocks on wrong door, she redirects them to their apartment
2. Teaching player their apartment number and floor
3. Explaining the building rules (no noise after 10pm, recycling in the courtyard)
4. Offering tea and teaching "yes/no/please/thank you"
5. Asking where the player is from and reacting with genuine curiosity
6. Teaching numbers 1-10 when explaining the intercom code
7. Showing player how to read the mailbox numbers
8. Giving player a spare key and explaining "lose it and I will not replace it"
9. Return visit: asking if player ate (teaching "I am hungry/full")
10. Return visit: teaching player to introduce themselves properly
11. Mission: deliver jam to apartment 7 (teaching floor numbers and knocking etiquette)
12. Mission: tell her what Dmitry Petrovich's music sounded like last night (teaching descriptions)
13. Mission: find out if the post arrived (teaching "is there/there is no")
14. Casual: showing player her plants and naming them (colors, adjectives)
15. Casual: talking about her daughter in Voronezh (family words, city names)
16. Chapter 2 reference: asking if player went to the park yet, mentions Artyom
17. Chapter 3 reference: recommending Fatima's stall at the market
18. Reaction to player making a grammar mistake — gentle correction, no embarrassment
19. Reaction to player making unexpected progress — quiet pride, no fuss
20. Seasonal: commenting on weather changing, teaching basic weather phrases
21. Late game: asking player to read a note for her (reversing teacher/student dynamic)
22. Late game: telling player about why she stayed in Malinov when everyone else left
23. Final chapter: worried when player is in trouble, sends them to the police station herself

#### СТЕПАН (Styopan)
**Role:** Player's upstairs neighbor, age 24, student at the same university
**Age:** 24
**Portrait cue:** Tall, tired-looking, always in a university hoodie. Headphones usually around his neck, never on his ears.

**Who he is:**
Styopan is from Yekaterinburg. He has been in Malinov for two years and has developed the specific world-weariness of someone who chose to study here and is not sure that was right. He is not unfriendly — he is simply low-energy. He speaks to you like you are already an acquaintance, which is its own kind of warmth.

He is studying economics and finds it boring. He plays video games badly and talks about them constantly. He knows where everything in the building is and what is broken and why. He has a complicated relationship with Galina Ivanovna (she once confiscated his delivery package for making noise, and he has never fully forgiven her).

He will occasionally appear at the wrong moment and say exactly the wrong thing in a way that is accidentally useful to the player.

**What he knows about the other NPCs:**
- Tells player about Artyom in the park: "He's always there. I think the park is his apartment."
- Mentions Lena at the cafe: "Best coffee in town. Which is not saying much."
- Doesn't know anyone at the train station personally but has strong opinions about the schedule.

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting in the hallway — startled to see a new face
2. Explaining that the elevator is broken (and has been for six months)
3. Teaching player the word for stairs, floor, door
4. Asking player what courses they are taking (teaching subjects and schedules)
5. Complaining about Galina Ivanovna — player learns conflict language
6. Asking player to translate something from English on his phone (role reversal)
7. Mission: slide note under neighbor's door (teaching "please be quiet")
8. Mission: check if anyone is using the laundry room
9. Casual: showing player his video game and asking if they know it
10. Casual: explaining Russian university culture vs. the player's home country
11. Casual: complaining about the cafeteria (teaching food words negatively)
12. Chapter 2: asking if player has been outside yet, slightly judging them
13. Chapter 3: recommending the cafe but getting Lena's name wrong ("the one with the eyes")
14. Chapter 4: player sees him stressed about an exam — teaches "I have to, I need to"
15. Teaching player slang the moment Galina Ivanovna is out of earshot
16. Reaction to player learning a new word: "Oh, she taught you that?"
17. Reaction to player being late back: mild concern disguised as annoyance
18. Reference to the train station: "I went home once. The train took nine hours."
19. Late game: admitting he is glad they moved in — but said very casually
20. Late game: asking player to help him write a note in English to someone
21. Final chapter: the one who tells player there is a problem and they need to go to the police station

### PARK — Location 2

---

#### АРТЁМ ВОРОНОВ (Artyom Voronov)
**Role:** The young man who is always in the park, informal guide to the town
**Age:** 21
**Portrait cue:** Lean, quick eyes, usually sitting on the back of a bench rather than the seat. Always has a paperback book in his back pocket that he is not currently reading.

**Who he is:**
Artyom grew up in Malinov and has complicated feelings about still being there. He finished school two years ago and has been working at the small grocery kiosk near the park entrance while figuring out what to do next. He reads a lot — mostly Soviet-era adventure novels and whatever he can find translated into Russian. He is curious about the world in the way of someone who has not left their town yet.

He speaks clearly and relatively slowly, which makes him the best conversationalist the player will encounter early on. He is not performing patience — he is genuinely interested in the player and wants to understand them. He will ask questions the player cannot yet answer, and find this amusing rather than frustrating.

He knows everyone in this town. Not in the social way — in the way of someone who has watched the same streets for twenty-one years.

**What he knows about the other NPCs:**
- Galina Ivanovna: "She fed me every summer when I was small. I still owe her about forty jars of jam."
- Fatima at the market: "Buy from her, not from the guy next to her. Trust me."
- Lena at the cafe: "She's been there forever. She knows things about this town she'll never tell you."
- Konstantin at the station: "He's all right. Just don't ask him things he doesn't know — he'll make something up."

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — he notices player looking lost on the park path
2. Teaching player basic directions: left, right, straight ahead, here, there
3. Describing the park — benches, fountain, trees (adjectives, simple present)
4. Asking player yes/no questions about their life — modeling question structure
5. Teaching player how to describe the weather using what they observe
6. Mission: find the old man playing chess (teaches "where is" + location words)
7. Mission: deliver a message to someone at the far end of the park
8. Mission: help him understand something a tourist asked him in English
9. Casual: showing player which path leads to the cafe, which to the market
10. Casual: talking about his book and what it is about (narrative tense practice)
11. Casual: pointing out regulars in the park and describing them (descriptions)
12. Chapter 3 bridge: walks player toward the cafe, introduces them to Lena indirectly
13. Chapter 3 bridge: mentions Fatima's market specifically — "Tell her Artyom sent you"
14. Reaction to player using a direction correctly — genuine delight
15. Reaction to player describing something in Russian unprompted
16. Rainy day variant: player finds him under the covered area with a different mood
17. Seasonal variant: summer afternoon vs. early autumn afternoon — different energy
18. Cross-reference: asks if Galina Ivanovna is feeding player properly
19. Cross-reference: tells player about the train station before player has unlocked it
20. Late game: reveals he has applied to a university in another city
21. Late game: asks player what their city is like — player describes home in Russian
22. Final chapter: concerned when player is in trouble, offers to come with them (can't)
23. Secret background: the book in his pocket is one Galina Ivanovna gave him years ago

#### ТАМАРА АНДРЕЕВНА БОРИСОВА (Tamara Andreyevna Borisova)
**Role:** Retired schoolteacher, regular park visitor, chess-adjacent
**Age:** 71
**Portrait cue:** Upright posture, warm eyes behind small rectangular glasses. Always in a coat regardless of the temperature. Often sitting near the chess tables, watching.

**Who she is:**
Tamara Andreyevna taught Russian language and literature for 35 years. She retired six years ago and now she comes to the park every day because routine is what keeps her sharp. She is not melancholy about retirement — she is quite satisfied. She taught well. She left something behind.

She will immediately recognize the player as a language learner and will feel the professional reflex to help. But she does this gently, in the style of someone who has spent decades finding the right way to explain the same thing to thirty different people. She gives the player the sense that making mistakes is a normal and temporary condition.

She knows a great deal about the history of Malinov and will share it unprompted but not boringly.

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — greets player with formal Russian, then adjusts when they look confused
2. Teaching player formal vs. informal greetings
3. Explaining that she was a teacher — player learns job words
4. Mission: she has lost her reading glasses somewhere in the park
5. Mission: she wants player to describe what the fountain looks like in Russian
6. Casual: talking about the seasons in Russia (comparative adjectives)
7. Casual: explaining the chess game she is watching without teaching chess
8. Cross-reference: mentions Galina Ivanovna as "a woman with a good heart and very strong opinions"
9. Cross-reference: knows Artyom's mother and asks after him
10. Teaching player formal address forms (Ivanovna, Andreyevna — why names work this way)
11. Reaction when player uses formal address correctly — visible approval
12. Teaching colors using the flowers planted near the fountain
13. Correcting a grammar mistake with a complete explanation, warm not clinical
14. Explaining a Russian saying and what it actually means
15. Chapter 3 crossover: mentions she sometimes goes to the cafe on Saturdays
16. Telling player about the town's history — old names, what was here before
17. Seasonal variant: autumn — talks about the trees, nostalgia, Russian poetry (first lines)
18. Late game: asks player to read something for her — she wants to hear their Russian
19. Late game: tells player they remind her of a particular student she had years ago
20. Final chapter: if player is in trouble, she is the one who tells them to stay calm

### CAFE — Location 3

---

#### ЛЕНА МИХАЙЛОВА (Lena Mikhailova)
**Role:** Barista and partial owner of the cafe, has been there since before you were born (in her opinion)
**Age:** 34
**Portrait cue:** Short dark hair, precise eyeliner, moves fast behind the counter. Apron with a small hand-embroidered flower on the pocket — she did it herself, though she'd deny caring about it.

**Who she is:**
Lena grew up in Malinov, left for Moscow at 19 to study hospitality, returned at 26 when her mother got sick, never left again. The cafe was supposed to be temporary. It has been eight years. She has made her peace with this in the specific way of someone who hasn't entirely.

She is sharp and impatient with people who can't decide what they want. She is unexpectedly patient with people who are genuinely trying. The player gets the second version of her almost immediately, which surprises them.

She has opinions about everything and will share them if you give her thirty seconds of uninterrupted listening. She notices things. She has watched this town long enough to have a complete mental model of everyone in it.

She makes the best coffee in Malinov. She knows it.

**What she knows about the other NPCs:**
- Artyom: "He comes in for tea he can't afford. I charge him half. Don't tell him."
- Galina Ivanovna: "She has never once sat down. She always orders to go. I think sitting feels like giving up to her."
- Fatima from the market: "My supplier for cardamom. We have an arrangement."
- Konstantin from the station: "He came in last week and asked for something 'exotic.' I gave him an oat milk latte. He's been back four times."

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — player tries to order, it goes wrong, she fixes it without embarrassment
2. Teaching player how to order: "I want... please... thank you"
3. Teaching the menu — drinks and their names
4. Teaching prices and numbers 10-100 in the context of paying
5. Explaining the difference between hot and cold, large and small
6. Mission: deliver a to-go order to someone at one of the outside tables
7. Mission: find out what the regular customer at the window seat usually orders (she's testing player)
8. Mission: player is asked to describe a complicated order — gets it wrong — Lena teaches the correction
9. Casual: asking where player is from, comparing Russia to their country
10. Casual: explaining what is good on the menu and what she would not recommend
11. Casual: telling player about Moscow without much nostalgia
12. Teaching polite request forms: "Could I have... Would it be possible..."
13. Accusative case — introduced naturally as "what do you want" (no grammar label)
14. Reaction when player orders correctly the first time without prompting
15. Reaction when player tries to use a slang word they got from Styopan
16. Cross-reference: mentions Artyom, tells player to say hello for her
17. Cross-reference: asks if player has been to the market yet, mentions Fatima
18. Rainy day variant: the cafe is full, Lena is stressed, dialogue reflects it
19. Afternoon variant: quiet period, Lena is more expansive, tells player more
20. Late game: admits she likes having player in here — it's different
21. Late game: teaches player how to complain about something politely
22. Final chapter: player comes to cafe under stress — she notices immediately and reacts

#### БОРИС ГЕННАДЬЕВИЧ КРАВЦОВ (Boris Gennadyevich Kravtsov)
**Role:** Regular at the window seat, retired engineer, reads the same newspaper
**Age:** 67
**Portrait cue:** Grey mustache, reading glasses on a cord, large hands. The newspaper is always from three days ago. He has not noticed.

**Who he is:**
Boris Gennadyevich comes to the cafe every morning at 9:15. He has done this for eleven years. He does not talk to people at the cafe normally — he is there to read his newspaper in peace. The player is the first person to break this pattern, and he responds to this with a kind of gruff bewilderment that gradually softens.

He was an engineer at the water treatment plant for thirty years. He knows how everything in this town works underground — the pipes, the cables, the systems no one thinks about. He is privately pleased when anyone asks.

He is not warm in the way of Galina Ivanovna or Artyom. He is dry and economical and occasionally says something that is, accidentally, very funny. The player will come to enjoy him.

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — player accidentally takes his seat (there is no sign; he considers the sign unnecessary)
2. Teaching player "excuse me, I'm sorry, my mistake"
3. Explaining what the newspaper says — teaches news vocabulary, dates, events
4. Mission: player must find and bring today's newspaper from the kiosk
5. Mission: Boris wants to know what the weather report says on player's phone
6. Casual: explaining what the town was like twenty years ago
7. Casual: telling player about his engineering work in terms they can follow
8. Cross-reference: knows Tamara Andreyevna from the park, mentions her
9. Cross-reference: opinion on the train station: "Konstantin runs it like he invented trains"
10. Teaching player numbers in the context of the newspaper's date
11. Reaction when player uses a formal phrase correctly — single approving nod
12. Reaction when player mispronounces something — he will say it back correctly twice, slowly
13. Afternoon visit variant: Boris is not there, player has to ask Lena where he is
14. Rainy day variant: Boris is in a different seat (it's near a window; that window leaks)
15. Teaching player time expressions: morning, afternoon, always, sometimes
16. Explaining how to say "every day" and demonstrating with his own life
17. Late game: Boris reveals he has a daughter who lives abroad — player is the first foreigner he has talked to
18. Late game: asks player if their country has good engineers (he means it as a compliment)
19. Final chapter: player finds him reading about something relevant to their trouble — foreshadowing
20. Secret: the newspaper is always three days old because he reads each one for three days before buying the next

### MARKET — Location 4

---

#### ФАТИМА ИСМАИЛОВА (Fatima Ismailova)
**Role:** Market vendor, best produce stall, the person who will not be bargained with
**Age:** 45
**Portrait cue:** Strong face, colorful headscarf, hands that move constantly — arranging, weighing, stacking. A small handwritten price list on a cardboard sign.

**Who she is:**
Fatima is from Dagestan. She has been in Malinov for twelve years. She came with her husband, who was supposed to be working here temporarily. He went back. She stayed — the stall was doing well, her children were settled in school. The temporary became permanent. She has no regret about it, or if she does, she keeps it to herself.

She is brisk, honest, and extremely effective at communicating across a language barrier because she has been doing it her whole working life. She will teach the player vocabulary not as a lesson but as a transaction requirement. She will not sell you something until you have said what you want in Russian.

She is fair and does not inflate prices for outsiders. She is aware of every vendor in the market and has a clear hierarchy of who is trustworthy.

She has a dry humor that takes the player a few visits to notice.

**What she knows about the other NPCs:**
- Galina Ivanovna: buys bread from her every Tuesday; they have a standing arrangement
- Artyom: "That boy sent you. I can tell."
- Lena from the cafe: "We have a cardamom arrangement. She pays on time. This is rare."
- Konstantin from the station: "He bought two kilos of carrots last week and did not know why. I did not ask."

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — player approaches, she waits, player has to figure out how to ask
2. Teaching names of vegetables and fruit in the context of buying them
3. Teaching quantities: one, half a kilo, two kilos
4. Teaching colors in the context of produce: red peppers, green apples, yellow lemons
5. Teaching numbers 10-100 through prices
6. Mission: player must buy a specific list of items (reading comprehension + shopping)
7. Mission: player must compare prices at two stalls and report back
8. Mission: delivery — take this package to the woman at the end of the row
9. Casual: explaining where different produce comes from
10. Casual: commenting on the weather's effect on what's in season
11. Cross-reference: mentions Galina Ivanovna sent her greetings (if player has met her)
12. Cross-reference: asks if Artyom is still sitting in that park doing nothing
13. Teaching player how to say something is fresh, ripe, good quality
14. Teaching player how to say they don't want something (politely declining)
15. Reaction when player names produce without prompting — visible approval
16. Reaction when player over-pays accidentally — she corrects it and gives change
17. Teaching "how much does this cost" and the full payment interaction
18. Afternoon variant: near closing, she is moving quickly — shorter dialogue
19. Rainy day variant: she has moved the stall slightly; player has to find her
20. Late game: player asks for something not on the stall; she directs them elsewhere
21. Late game: tells player about Dagestan unprompted — she rarely talks about herself
22. Final chapter: gives player provisions before they go to the station, no charge

**Role:** Young vendor a few stalls down from Fatima, 19, trying to run his uncle's stall
**Age:** 19
**Portrait cue:** Floppy hair, slightly-too-big apron, expression of someone who is improvising constantly.

Misha's uncle left him in charge of the household goods stall three months ago and has not come back yet. Misha is not angry about this — he is philosophically confused. He and Artyom know each other (same age, same town) and their dynamic is affectionate and competitive.

### TRAIN STATION — Location 5

---

#### КОНСТАНТИН ДМИТРИЕВИЧ НЕЧАЕВ (Konstantin Dmitriyevich Nechayev)
**Role:** Station master / ticket officer, formal, proud of the station, reliable
**Age:** 52
**Portrait cue:** Uniform cap, pressed collar, reading glasses he refuses to wear unless he thinks no one is watching. Sign on his window that says "Information" in Cyrillic.

**Who he is:**
Konstantin Dmitriyevich has worked at Malinov station for 24 years. He takes enormous quiet pride in the fact that the Malinov-to-Tver train has not been late on his watch more than seven times. (It has been late more than seven times. He has a different accounting system.)

He is formal by nature and by profession. He addresses everyone by name and patronymic if he knows it. He speaks in complete sentences. He does not abbreviate. This makes him an excellent teacher of formal Russian, though he would not describe himself that way.

He is not cold — he is precise. When he smiles it is small and real. He likes travelers. He has never gone anywhere, but he knows the schedules of every train that has passed through and imagines that he has traveled all of them.

He will, if player proves competent, start to treat them as someone who understands how a train station works. This is his version of warmth.

**What he knows about the other NPCs:**
- Boris Gennadyevich from the cafe: "A reliable man. Three days behind on everything, but reliable."
- Artyom: "I see him sometimes on the platform, just watching the trains. I don't ask."
- Fatima: the carrot purchase.
- Galina Ivanovna: "She sent me a pie when I was ill last winter. I have not forgotten."

**20+ distinct interaction contexts:**
1. First meeting — player needs to understand a train schedule
2. Teaching player how to ask "what time does the train leave"
3. Teaching player how to read a timetable (numbers, times, days)
4. Teaching formal direction language: platform 1, turn left, second window
5. Teaching player how to buy a ticket: destination, time, one-way/return
6. Mission: find out if a specific train is on time
7. Mission: deliver a notice to platform 2 (teaches directions inside a building)
8. Mission: help a confused traveler find their train
9. Casual: explaining the different trains and where they go (geography)
10. Casual: telling player about a notable event at the station (date/time language)
11. Teaching future tense through train arrivals: "the train will arrive at..."
12. Teaching "I need to be at X by Y time" — time pressure language
13. Cross-reference: mentions Galina Ivanovna's pie unprompted
14. Cross-reference: describes Artyom watching trains — player gets new perspective on him
15. Reaction when player uses formal address correctly — the small real smile
16. Reaction when player mispronounces the destination — corrects twice, officially
17. Afternoon variant: busier, shorter responses — player learns to be concise
18. Late evening variant: quiet station, Konstantin is almost reflective
19. Teaching player how to say their train has been cancelled and what to do
20. Late game: reveals he has memorized the schedules of trains he will never take
21. Late game: asks player where they are going next in life — future tense practice
22. Final chapter: player is in a hurry; Konstantin is calm and efficient — teaches urgency language

#### НАДЯ (Nadya)
**Role:** Young woman at the ticket kiosk, Konstantin's assistant, 23, aspirations elsewhere
**Age:** 23
**Portrait cue:** Bright eyes, hair in a practical braid, lipstick that does not match the uniform requirements. Small earphones perpetually around her neck.

**Who she is:**
Nadya wants to go to Saint Petersburg. She has wanted this for five years. She has saved enough money three separate times and spent it on something unavoidable each time. She is not tragic about this — she believes it will happen. She just doesn't know when.

She is the friendliest person at the station and the fastest talker. She speaks in a mix of formal station Russian and casual Russian and does not flag the transitions. Player will have to keep up.

She is interested in the player immediately — someone who came from outside is proof that the outside exists and is reachable.

6. Casual: asking player about their home city in detail (comparative language)
7. Casual: telling player about Saint Petersburg — her knowledge of it is encyclopedic
8. Cross-reference: mentions Artyom's habit of watching trains with quiet sympathy
9. Cross-reference: tells player about Lena at the cafe (she goes there on days off)
10. Teaching player how to ask "has the train arrived yet"

### POLICE STATION — Location 6

#### ЛЕЙТЕНАНТ ВИКТОР АЛЕКСАНДРОВИЧ ОРЛОВ (Lieutenant Viktor Aleksandrovich Orlov)
**Role:** Police officer, formal, competent, not unfriendly but his job requires him not to show it
**Age:** 38
**Portrait cue:** Uniform, short haircut, neutral expression that is not hostile. Very good posture. A pen always behind his right ear.

**Who he is:**

## Chapter arcs

### Chapter 1 — Arrival
The player arrives with nothing: no Russian, no bearings, no social network. The apartment building is the whole world. Galina Ivanovna makes it navigable. Styopan makes it livable. The player ends the chapter able to say who they are, where they live, and what they need.

Story beat: player is trying to find their apartment. Gets it wrong twice. Finally finds it. Galina Ivanovna is in the hallway. She doesn't make it a big deal. She just helps.

Unlock gate: Galina Ivanovna tells player the park is worth visiting. She mentions Artyom by name. "He's a good boy. He knows where things are."

### Chapter 2 — First steps
The town becomes something more than walls. The park opens the world spatially — player can now move, describe, orient. Artyom shows them the shape of the town. Tamara Andreyevna gives them their first sense of Russian culture beyond survival.

Story beat: player gets turned around in the park. Artyom finds them, not quite by accident. They have their first real conversation. It is mostly pointing and nodding, but something is communicated.

Unlock gate: Artyom explicitly tells player about the cafe and the market. "If you're going to be here all semester, you need to know where to eat." He gives them directions in Russian. They mostly follow them.

### Chapter 3 — Needing things
The player has to interact with the world commercially. The cafe and market require them to ask for things, pay for things, describe things. Mistakes have social consequences (mild ones: embarrassment, confusion) but also social rewards.

Story beat: player attempts to order at the cafe. Gets it almost right. Lena corrects them and gives them what they wanted anyway. Player feels, for the first time, that they can do this.

Second story beat: at the market, Fatima will not sell without being asked properly. Player has to figure out how to ask. She waits. She does not help until they try. When they get it right, she says "хорошо" ("good") and means it.

Unlock gate: something is wrong. The player needs to go to the train station. Styopan is the one who tells them. It is not dramatic — just a practical problem that requires going somewhere new. "You might need to go to the station. Konstantin there can help."

### Chapter 4 — Something goes wrong
The player has lost a document. Or a bag. Something official needs to happen. The train station and police station are where formal Russian lives. The chapter is about navigating bureaucracy in a foreign language — which is something most language learners will eventually face in real life.

Story beat at station: Konstantin helps the player track down what happened to their document. He is efficient and calm. Player has to describe the problem, give times, give dates.

Story beat at police station: Viktor takes the report. Player has to tell the story of what happened in past tense. Alina processes the paperwork. The situation is resolved — not dramatically, just resolved. The player realizes they handled it. In Russian.

Final beat: player returns to Galina Ivanovna. She asks how it went. Player tells her. She nods and goes back to her watering can. "Good. Have you eaten?"

The semester is not over. But the player has arrived.

## Vocabulary scope documentation

*(Supporting the curriculum-map.md that curriculum-designer will write)*

**Location 1 — Apartment building:**
Greetings (привет, здравствуйте, добрый день), farewells, self-introduction (меня зовут, я из, я студент/студентка), numbers 1-10, floor/apartment vocabulary, polite words (пожалуйста, спасибо, извините), basic yes/no (да/нет), "I don't understand" (я не понимаю)

**Location 2 — Park:**
Directions (налево, направо, прямо, здесь, там), adjectives (big/small, new/old, beautiful), colors (basic 6), simple present tense (я иду, он сидит), question words (где, что, как, кто), weather basics, describing people and places

**Location 3 — Cafe:**
Ordering vocabulary (я хочу, дайте мне, можно), drink and food names, prices (numbers 10-100), accusative case unlabelled (I want coffee → кофе), polite request forms (можно мне, не могли бы вы), "how much" (сколько стоит)

**Location 4 — Market:**
Produce vocabulary, quantity expressions (один, полкило, два кило), more colors, adjectives for quality (свежий, спелый, хороший), "I'll take this/that," paying and change, "I don't want/need" (мне не нужно)

**Location 5 — Train station:**
Time expressions (в котором часу, во сколько, через час), future tense (поезд прибудет в), direction in buildings (первый путь, второй вагон), ticket vocabulary, days of the week, formal instruction-following language

**Location 6 — Police station:**
Formal register (вы vs ты established explicitly), past tense narrative (я потерял/а, это произошло), document vocabulary (паспорт, документ, адрес), case awareness (genitive after у меня нет), "I need" (мне нужно + infinitive), emergency phrases, formal goodbyes

Reinforcement chain: every vocabulary domain introduced in location N appears in at least one interaction at location N+1.
