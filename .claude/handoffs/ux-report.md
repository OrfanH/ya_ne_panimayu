## Verification Checklist

### 1. Does Galina feel like a real person, not a language textbook?

**PASS.** Galina has personality, agency, and believable mannerisms.

Evidence:
- Physical presence: watering can, gestures at numbers, key ring weight, offering soup/jam as character acts
- Conversational tic: "Такие правила" appears twice—suggests someone thinking in order/systems
- Patience without condescension: never sighs, breaks confusion into smaller pieces
- Social judgment: emphasizes "здравствуйте" over "привет" for neighbors—cares about respect, not grammar rules alone
- Non-verbal teaching: approval via silent nods, disapproval about eating, warmth through actions not effusive language

Not a textbook. A person.

---

### 2. Are there 8+ distinct visit variations?

**PASS.** 11 discrete variations (opening + 10 named).

1. Opening beat — identity and apartment
2. Variation 1: wrong door, redirect
3. Variation 2: self-introduction (name + origin)
4. Variation 3: geography question
5. Variation 4: tea and politeness (да/нет forms)
6. Variation 5: intercom code
7. Variation 6: mailbox numbers
8. Variation 7: spare key responsibility
9. Variation 8: return check-in (did you eat)
10. Variation 9: jam delivery mission
11. Variation 10: neighbor register lesson (привет vs. здравствуйте)

Each distinct in context and learning goal. Not repetitive.

---

### 3. Does vocabulary appear naturally in context, not drilled artificially?

**PASS with curriculum coverage note.** Embedded contextually through action and situation.

Evidence:
- Numbers appear when counting on fingers, listing floors
- "Меня зовут" modeled in actual introduction, not extracted
- "Я из" asked when meeting new resident
- Politeness emerges from offering tea
- Building vocabulary tied to physical systems (intercom, mailboxes, keys)
- "Вы ели" is genuine check-in, not grammar prompt
- Register distinction taught as social logic, not grammar

Linguist confirmed: no artificial drilling.

**Curriculum coverage gap:**
- "до свидания" (goodbye) — listed but NOT in dialogue
- "извините" (excuse/sorry) — listed but NOT in dialogue

Both are A1 core. Should appear in: opening (apology), closing (farewell), or Variation 1 (wrong door).

This is coverage incomplete, not content defective.

---

### 4. Is there a clear learning progression from variation 1 to 10?

**PASS.** Intentional, scaffolded progression.

Skill ladder:
- Var 1–2: survival navigation (apartment numbers, names)
- Var 3–4: social interaction (origin, politeness register)
- Var 5–6: building systems (intercom, mailboxes)
- Var 7–8: responsibility (keys, social care)
- Var 9: mission comprehension (jam delivery)
- Var 10: cultural context (register when/why, neighbor relationships)

Each builds on prior vocabulary in new context. Coherent, not random.

---

### 5. Are player choices meaningful (not just "correct" vs "wrong")?

**PASS.** Choices have social and learning consequences.

Evidence:
- Var 1 Choice C ("Спасибо"): off-topic, not wrong. Teaches politeness ≠ always relevant
- Var 4 Choice C ("Да" alone): models proper politeness form by repetition, not punishment
- Var 10 Choice B ("Привет"): socially meaningful. Galina explains why (friends only), not just "wrong"
- Var 7 Choice B ("Спасибо"): consequence is action (she holds key), not score
- "Я не понимаю" choices: trigger modeling, respect signal of confusion

Consequences teach, not punish.

---

### 6. Does the unlock sequence feel achievable and fair?

**PASS.** Transparent, achievable, respectful.

Logic:
- Trigger: after Var 1 + Var 2
- Success: greeting + name + number in one exchange
- Feedback: specific (missing greeting: "Start from beginning")
- Partial credit: two-of-three = "Good. But —"
- Failure: silence = model once more, then "Next time"

Fair because:
- All three elements practiced in Variations 1–2
- Multiple attempts allowed
- No hidden criteria
- "Next time" respects learner, not judge

---

### 7. Does the conversation respect the player's intelligence?

**PASS.** Galina teaches by modeling and action, never talks down.

Evidence:
- No diminutives or baby-talk
- Physical context assumed, not explained (key ring = responsibility)
- Rules presented as apartment facts, not lessons: "После десяти — тишина. Такие правила."
- Jam mission: trusts player to infer (real favor, neighbor matters, models greeting)
- Register lesson: explains *why* (привет for friends, not "wrong")
- Silence as teaching: demonstrates, waits, "Your turn"

Assumes player can infer from action and context.
