- до свидания · goodbye · phrase
- меня зовут · my name is · phrase

- я студент / студентка · I am a student · noun phrase
- пожалуйста · please / you're welcome · adverb
- спасибо · thank you · interjection
- извините · excuse me / sorry · interjection
- да · yes · particle
- нет · no · particle
- я не понимаю · I don't understand · phrase
- один, два, три, четыре, пять, шесть, семь, восемь, девять, десять · 1–10 · numeral
- этаж · floor · noun
- квартира · apartment · noun
- дверь · door · noun
- ключ · key · noun

**Words reappearing from previous location:** none (first location)

**Structures introduced:**
- Greeting someone depending on time of day and familiarity
- Introducing yourself with name, origin, and role
- Saying you don't understand and asking someone to repeat
- Using numbers 1–10 to state floor and apartment number

**Unlock gate — what the player must demonstrate:**

- красивый · beautiful · adjective
- красный, синий, зелёный, белый, чёрный, жёлтый · red, blue, green, white, black, yellow · adjective
- я иду · I am going · verb phrase
- он сидит / она сидит · he/she is sitting · verb phrase
- тепло / холодно · warm / cold · adverb
- дождь · rain · noun
- солнце · sun · noun

**Words reappearing from Location 1 in new context:**
- здравствуйте / привет — used to greet Artyom and Tamara Andreyevna (new people, same greeting contrast formal vs. informal)
- да / нет — used to answer directional questions ("Is this the right path? — Да.")
- numbers 1–10 — Artyom's chess table numbers; describing position in the park layout

**Structures introduced:**
- Asking and giving directions using left/right/straight
- Describing what someone looks like or where they are using adjectives and "here/there"
- Talking about basic weather

**Unlock gate — what the player must demonstrate:**
Give correct directions to at least one NPC within the park (using налево/направо/прямо + location word) and describe one person or place using an adjective.

## Location 3: Cafe (Кафе)

**Words introduced:**
- я хочу · I want · verb phrase
- дайте мне · give me · imperative phrase
- можно · may I / is it possible · modal particle
- можно мне · may I have · polite request phrase
- не могли бы вы · could you please · formal request phrase
- кофе, чай, вода, сок · coffee, tea, water, juice · noun
- хлеб, суп, салат · bread, soup, salad · noun
- сколько стоит · how much does it cost · phrase
- десять–сто (10–100) · ten through one hundred · numeral
- рубль / рублей · rouble / roubles · noun
- счёт · the bill · noun

**Words reappearing from Location 2 in new context:**
- большой / маленький — ordering a large or small coffee ("большой кофе, пожалуйста")
- красивый — Boris Gennadyevich describes a view from the window; player has the word
- как — "Как вас зовут?" from Lena; player recognizes the question word
- directions (здесь/там) — locating a free table ("Там есть место?")
- numbers 1–10 — reused as the base layer for prices in the 10–100 range

**Structures introduced:**
- Ordering food and drink using three request levels: я хочу / можно мне / дайте мне
- Asking the price and understanding a number as an answer
- Paying and receiving change (numbers 10–100 in a real transaction)
- Unlabelled accusative: the object of "I want" naturally takes accusative form

**Unlock gate — what the player must demonstrate:**
Order at least one item using a complete request phrase, ask the price, and state the correct number when paying.

- килограмм / кило, полкило · kilogram, half a kilo · noun/phrase
- свежий · fresh · adjective
- спелый · ripe · adjective

- я возьму это / я возьму вот это · I'll take this / I'll take that · phrase
- мне не нужно · I don't need / want · phrase
- сдача · change (money) · noun
- дорого / дёшево · expensive / cheap · adverb

**Words reappearing from Location 3 in new context:**
- numbers 10–100 — prices at market stalls (same number range, new transactional context: weight × price)
- можно — "Можно посмотреть?" (Can I look?) — same particle, new request
- рубль / рублей — paying Fatima; change calculation
- сколько стоит — asking stall prices; player now initiates it without prompting
- хлеб — Misha's stall has bread; word recurs in a supply rather than ordering context
- большой / маленький — choosing a larger or smaller quantity

**Structures introduced:**
- Naming a quantity and a product together: "полкило картошки"
- Accepting and declining a purchase politely
- Comparing two items using adjectives (этот свежее — this one is fresher)
- Paying and checking change from a market stall

**Unlock gate — what the player must demonstrate:**
Complete a full market transaction — name a product, state a quantity, ask the price, pay, and use мне не нужно to decline at least one unwanted item.

## Location 5: Train Station (Железнодорожный вокзал)

**Words introduced:**
- в котором часу / во сколько · at what time · question phrase
- через час / через два часа · in one hour / in two hours · time phrase
- поезд · train · noun
- прибудет / отправляется · will arrive / departs · verb (future)
- билет · ticket · noun
- платформа / путь · platform / track · noun
- вагон · carriage / car · noun
- понедельник–воскресенье · Monday–Sunday · noun (days)
- направление · direction (of travel) · noun
- вы (formal pronoun use made explicit) · you (formal) · pronoun

**Words reappearing from Location 4 in new context:**
- numbers 1–10 — platform and carriage numbers ("второй вагон, первый путь")
- дорого — ticket price comparison; player may comment on cost
- хороший — Nadya uses it describing a train route ("хороший маршрут")
- directions (налево/направо/прямо) — finding platforms inside the station
- сколько стоит — asking ticket price; exact same phrase, official context

**Structures introduced:**
- Asking and reading a timetable: time expressions with в + accusative hour
- Future tense: talking about what will happen (the train will arrive at…)
- Formal register: using вы instead of ты; player is explicitly prompted here
- Following compound instructions: "Go to window two, ask for a ticket to X, platform three"

**Unlock gate — what the player must demonstrate:**
Ask for a ticket to a named destination using вы (formal), state the day of travel, confirm the platform number, and repeat the departure time back to Konstantin correctly.

## Location 6: Police Station (Полицейский участок)

**Words introduced:**
- паспорт · passport · noun
- документ · document · noun
- адрес · address · noun
- я потерял / я потеряла · I lost (masc./fem.) · verb (past)
- это произошло · it happened · verb phrase (past)
- у меня нет + genitive · I don't have · negation phrase
- мне нужно + infinitive · I need to · phrase
- заявление · official statement / report · noun
- на помощь / помогите · help (emergency) · phrase
- до свидания (formal context) · formal goodbye · phrase

**Words reappearing from Location 5 in new context:**
- вы — police officers use вы exclusively; player now must sustain formal register throughout
- билет — mentioned when player explains travel plans or the incident; recurs as plot-relevant vocabulary
- адрес — player gave an address at the station (in transit context); now must give it formally on a form
- дни недели — stating what day the incident occurred ("это произошло в среду")
- направление — recurs when player describes where they were going when something happened
- через час — used in "come back in an hour" instructions from Alina

**Structures introduced:**
- Past tense narrative: telling the story of an event with я потерял/а + time and place
- Genitive after negation: у меня нет документа — first explicit case-as-system moment
- Formal request under pressure: the player must be clear and complete; vague answers stall the process
- Form-filling: reading a form field and producing the correct Russian answer

**Unlock gate — what the player must demonstrate:**
Narrate a past event in at least two sentences (what happened, when, where), produce a genitive form correctly in context (у меня нет + noun), and complete one form field correctly in Russian.
